Topic 1 - Fundamentals

Understanding the Concepts

There are some fundamental concepts in the delivery of online university subjects. These are derived from the type and demographics of the students, the nature of the subject matter, and the tools available.

These concepts are explored in detail, and the most important forces behind the online learning experience are examined.

Intended Learning

Participants will learn to define the online learning experience from the student’s perspective. The forces that influence student engagement will be explored and analysed. This subject will lay a foundation for how and why the various tools and techniques influence the learning outcomes and student satisfaction.

Threshold Concepts

When considering the most important discoverings of any topic, the threshold concepts are those which will irreversibly change the way you think about the subject from here on. The following are considered threshold concepts that, once understood, will forever change your understanding.

  • Students attending online are engaged mostly by expression and opinion, and less by facts and information.
  • Passion and enthusiasm of the mentor are often more valuable than expertise and knowledge.

Questions to Ponder

Consider the following questions as a guide to understanding this topic. Ask yourself how well you understand these principles and how satisfied you are that you can explain them to others.

  • What effect does a mentor’s enthusiasm have on the engagement level of students?
  • What are the factors of online learning that make it more detached than face to face?
  • What are the main differences between students interacting with each other compared to students interacting with mentors?

Considerations and Observations

As we explore communication theory take these considerations into account when forming your own opinions.

  • An online mentor has no real control over what the students do or when they do it, even during a live class.
  • The role of the mentor is not merely to disperse the knowledge, but to encourage the learning of it.
  • Students engaging with online content look for clues and guidance on the timing and sequence of their interaction.

Activity Task

To practice this topic and to embed its knowledge participants should conduct the following task. Do this in your own time when the opportunity arises.

Consider an online subject with which you are familiar. Review the break down of its content into topics and sections. Look at how this guides the student through the process of absorbing its content.

Decide how you might rearrange or otherwise deliver this content in a different sequence or in a new way to improve how the student might be engaged with it.
