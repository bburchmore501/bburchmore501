Topic 2 - Using the CMS

Working With the Online Portal

The online interactive portal is the main central medium between the mentor and the students. It is a vital link between the students and their content, but also between the mentor and their students. Most portals are all-encompassing, they include everything needed for the functions of online learning.

These systems are usually flexible and programmable, allowing the mentor to design the interaction in many ways. This defines the user experience of the student and can greatly impact their involvement, satisfaction and learning outcomes.

Intended Learning

In this topic participants will learn about how the design and layout of the CMS/LMS can impact student outcomes. The user experience will be explored along with practical guidance on where and how to arrange elements and content within the CMS.

Participants will understand how students engage with CMS systems and will learn how best to maximise this.

Threshold Concepts

When considering the most important discoverings of any topic, the threshold concepts are those which will irreversibly change the way you think about the subject from here on. The following are considered threshold concepts that, once understood, will forever change your understanding.

  • Ease of access and linking to information is an important criterion for student engagement.
  • Students rely on context and compartmentalisation to quickly find discussions to interact with.

Questions to Ponder

Consider the following questions as a guide to understanding this topic. Ask yourself how well you understand these principles and how satisfied you are that you can explain them to others.

  • Why is a logical separation of forum discussions important?
  • What often happens when a student does not find what they are looking for fast enough?
  • What is a patience threshold and how does it affect CMS interaction?

Considerations and Observations

As we explore communication theory take these considerations into account when forming your own opinions.

  • Even the most basic CMS can be used to do what is required, as in most cases portals are unnecessarily complicated in their configuration.
  • Students will often download the raw data and information when they can to have a simplified offline version of the same content.
  • Most students will interact with group forums only rarely, and usually not for the entire subject period.

Activity Task

To practice this topic and to embed its knowledge students should conduct the following task. Do this in your own time when the opportunity arises.

Consider an online subject with which you are familiar. Review how the content is dispersed through the portal or CMS that it uses. Examine the steps that a student would need to take to access various content elements within the portal.

Identify three changes you could make that would lower the barriers to engagement for a student of that subject.

Include in your revision how the forums could be better arranged to more clearly define the topics to make them easier for students to find/interact with.
