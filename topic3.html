Topic 3 - Slide Decks

The Visual Presentation Aid

Slide decks are used to deliver a sequenced, managed flow of information to an online webinar presentation. This is quite different to the traditional role of slide decks used to accompany a live personal presentation in a room.

The role of the slide deck here requires different priorities, qualities and design considerations.

These qualities will define the value of the slide deck to the audience.

Intended Learning

Participants will learn the peculiar attributes of slide decks as they relate to the demands of online learning webinars. We will explore how to use these advantages to improve student attention during the webinars, and for effective use of the slide decks as a study aid.

Threshold Concepts

When considering the most important discoverings of any topic, the threshold concepts are those which will irreversibly change the way you think about the subject from here on. The following are considered threshold concepts that, once understood, will forever change your understanding.

  • Slide decks must be used to help a student visually cue to a specific discussion in the webinar.
  • Webinar slide decks break many rules regarding live presentation slide decks.

Questions to Ponder

Consider the following questions as a guide to understanding this topic. Ask yourself how well you understand these principles and how satisfied you are that you can explain them to others.

  • What is the ratio of processing visual versus audio information?
  • What is meant by the principle of primacy and recency and what role does it play in slide presentations?
  • Can you deliver your lesson even if students are not also watching your slides? If so, what is the job of those slides?

Considerations and Observations

As we explore communication theory take these considerations into account when forming your own opinions.

  • During review study, students refer to the slide deck of a webinar to gauge where in the recording a particular topic was covered.
  • High resolution graphics can take too long to broadcast during a webinar and should be avoided.
  • Transitions, movement and embedded video does not deliver well via a webinar.

Activity Task

To practice this topic and to embed its knowledge students should conduct the following task. Do this in your own time when the opportunity arises.

Consider a slide deck you have seen or built that was used for a live presentation. Review the differences and influencing factors that defined that slide deck for its intended purpose and audience.

Redesign that slide deck into a format to be used as an aid for an online webinar. Try to retain as much of the original message as possible, but in the context of a learning experience.
